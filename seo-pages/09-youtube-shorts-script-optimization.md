## 3. Search Intent Analysis

### What Problem the User Is Experiencing
Creators are investing hours into filming and editing Shorts, only to have them flop because of script-level issues. They want to catch problems *before* filming—at the planning stage—rather than discovering a weak hook or pacing issue after the video is already shot.

### Why Existing Advice or Tools Aren't Helping
- **Script templates are too generic:** "Hook → Body → Payoff" doesn't help with *your* specific idea
- **AI scriptwriters produce content, not feedback:** ChatGPT can write a script but can't tell you if it'll work as a Short
- **No way to test before filming:** Without a feedback loop, every shoot is a gamble
- **Post-hoc feedback is too late:** Learning your hook was weak after 48 hours of filming and editing is painful

Creators need to validate and optimize their content structure before they invest in production.

## 5. Product-Led Explanation

### How Shorta Works for This Use Case:

1. **Upload a Rough Draft**  
   You don't need a polished edit. Film a quick, low-effort version of your concept and upload the MP4 to Shorta. This is your test video.

2. **Automatic Script-Level Analysis**  
   Shorta watches your draft and identifies structural issues:
   - **Hook effectiveness:** Is the grabber in the first 1-2 seconds?
   - **Pacing analysis:** Does the script flow or stall mid-way?
   - **Clarity check:** Is the premise clear without extra context?
   - **Payoff review:** Does the ending match the hook's promise?

3. **Concrete, Actionable Feedback**  
   Even on a rough draft, you'll get notes like:
   > "0:00–0:03: Hook buries the lead. The interesting outcome appears at 0:08—move it to second 1."
   > "0:12–0:18: Pacing lags. This section doesn't advance the premise—cut or restructure."

4. **Storyboard Suggestions for Filming**  
   Shorta generates a revised storyboard:
   - Recommended scene order
   - Suggested hook rewrites
   - Sections to cut or expand
   - You film the real version with a proven structure
